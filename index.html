encrypted pastebin — dns-backed, zero trust

how it works

link-only — AES-256-GCM encrypted. key lives in the URL fragment, never sent to any server.

password — Argon2id + AES-256-GCM. encrypted with your password. no key in the URL.

burn — same as link-only but the paste self-destructs after first read.

deniable — two passwords, two contents, one ciphertext. give up the decoy password under coercion. no way to prove the real content exists.

public — encrypted at rest but key is stored alongside data. anyone with the link can read.

pastes are stored as DNS TXT records. the static page is the only way to decrypt them.
